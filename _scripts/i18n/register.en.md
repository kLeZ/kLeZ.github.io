## Language variety (British English, not American)
- Use **British spelling**: `-ise/-isation` (organise, realise, optimisation), `colour`,
  `behaviour`, `centre`, `licence` (noun) / `license` (verb), `defence`, `catalogue`,
  `analyse`, `programme` (except a *computer program*), `grey`.
- Use British/European vocabulary and idiom; **avoid Americanisms** and US slang
  (e.g. prefer *autumn* over *fall*, *flat* over *apartment*, *mobile* over *cell phone*,
  *full stop* over *period*, *maths* over *math*). Avoid US-specific cultural references
  and framing.
- Keep a neutral European sensibility. Do **not** inject opinions or editorialise — this is
  about the variety of English only, never about content.
